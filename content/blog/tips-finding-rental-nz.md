# 10 Tips for Finding a Rental in New Zealand

Finding a rental in New Zealand can be competitive, especially in popular cities like Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch. Here are our top tips:

## 1. Start Early
The rental market moves fast. Start searching at least 2-4 weeks before your move-in date.

## 2. Have Your Documents Ready
Landlords typically ask for: photo ID, references from previous landlords, proof of income, and a cover letter.

## 3. Use Multiple Platforms
Don't rely on just one website. Check TradeMe, Facebook groups, and AI-powered tools like RentFinder NZ.

## 4. Set a Realistic Budget
In Auckland, expect to pay $200-$400/week for a room. In smaller cities, $150-$250/week is more common.

## 5. Consider Flatting
Sharing a flat is the most affordable option for students and young professionals.

## 6. Check the Location
Use Google Maps to check commute times to your workplace or university.

## 7. Visit in Person
Always inspect the property before signing a lease. Photos can be misleading.

## 8. Read the Tenancy Agreement
Understand your rights under the Residential Tenancies Act. Bond is usually 4 weeks rent.

## 9. Know Your Rights
Landlords must provide a healthy home. Check for insulation, heating, and ventilation.
